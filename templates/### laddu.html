Laddu

Laddu is one of the most popular Indian sweets. It is a round, ball-shaped sweet made from chickpea flour, sugar, and ghee. Laddu is often served at festivals and celebrations, and it is also a popular gift.

History

The origins of Laddu can be traced back to ancient India. It is believed that Laddu was first made in the kitchens of the royal palaces. Over time, Laddu became popular among the common people and it is now enjoyed all over India.

Types of Laddu

There are many different types of Laddu, each with its own unique flavor and texture. Some of the most popular Laddu include:

  • Motichoor Laddu: This type of Laddu is made with tiny balls of boondi (chickpea flour) that are deep-fried and then coated in sugar syrup.
  • Besan Laddu: This type of Laddu is made with chickpea flour, sugar, and ghee. It is usually flavored with cardamom and nuts.
  • Rava Laddu: This type of Laddu is made with semolina, sugar, and ghee. It is often flavored with nuts and raisins.
  • Coconut Laddu: This type of Laddu is made with grated coconut, sugar, and ghee. It is often flavored with cardamom and nuts.

Recipes

There are many different recipes for Laddu. Here is a simple recipe for Motichoor Laddu:

  1. Ingredients:
    • 1 cup chickpea flour
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1/2 cup ghee
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 1/4 cup nuts (almonds, pistachios, cashews)
  2. Instructions:
    1. Heat the ghee in a pan.
    2. Add the chickpea flour and fry until it turns golden brown.
    3. Add the sugar and stir until it dissolves.
    4. Add the cardamom powder and nuts.
    5. Pour the mixture onto a greased plate and let it cool.
    6. Once the mixture has cooled, break it into small pieces and serve.
