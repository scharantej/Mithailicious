Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular Indian sweet that is made by deep-frying batter in a spiral shape and then soaking it in sugar syrup. Jalebi is often served hot and it is a popular snack or dessert.

History

The origins of Jalebi can be traced back to ancient India. It is believed that Jalebi was first made in the kitchens of the royal palaces. Over time, Jalebi became popular among the common people and it is now enjoyed all over India.

Types of Jalebi

There are many different types of Jalebi, each with its own unique flavor and texture. Some of the most popular Jalebi include:

  • Plain Jalebi: This is the most basic type of Jalebi. It is made with a simple batter of flour, sugar, and water.
  • Rabdi Jalebi: This type of Jalebi is made with a batter that is flavored with rabdi (a thick, sweetened milk).
